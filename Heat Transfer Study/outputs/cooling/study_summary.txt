Summer cooling selection process:
  Cooling recommendations now use constrained NSGA-II on the evaluated summer airflow sweep.
  NSGA-II settings                      : population 100, generations 500, seed 7
  Step 1: compute the inequality-constraint residuals listed below for every sampled flow point.
  Step 2: run NSGA-II with those residuals as hard constraints and the objective set listed below.
  Step 3: keep only fully summer-hard-safe points on the resulting Pareto front.
  Step 4: report one design point from that feasible Pareto set using the configured cooling report-priority order.
  No weighted penalty score is used.
  Summer hard-safe means all of the following hold simultaneously:
    1. the predicted network pressure drop stays below the assist-blower available static pressure at that airflow;
    2. pressure drop, substrate water loss, and hole velocity remain below their configured limits;
    3. bottom and top bed temperatures remain within their configured summer minima and maxima;
    4. bottom-top spread remains below the configured spread limit; and
    5. net Q_to_bed is negative, so the spot-cooled airflow is actually removing heat from the bed.

Perforation and moisture process:
  Per-hole release and moisture loss are evaluated explicitly for the aeration tubes.
  Source map                           : Frederickson et al. (2007) Table 3 particle-size data,
                                         Churchill-Bernstein hole convection, Lewis relation,
                                         and IAPWS saturation/latent-heat relations
  Perforation geometry                 : 100 holes/tube, 5.0 mm diameter, area per hole 0.00001963 m^2
  Perforation discharge coefficient    : 0.60
  Per-hole flow relation               : local released flow follows C_d A_h sqrt(2 rho Delta p)
  Hole-velocity relation               : local released flow / local perforation area
  Evaporation relation                 : m_dot = min(m_diffusion, m_capacity, m_heat)
  Diffusion limit                      : m_diffusion = h_m A (rho_v,sat(T_surface) - rho_v,bulk)
                                         the diffusion, carrying-capacity, and heat-limited
                                         evaporation rates are all evaluated in kg/s
  Heat-mass analogy                    : h_m = h / (rho c_p Le^(2/3)); h comes from the hole-scale convection correlation
  Sensible-to-latent cap               : m_heat = q_available / h_fg
  Moisture-state inputs                : RH 35%, surface temp 20.0 C, wetted area 612.297 m^2, Lewis factor 1.00
  Wetted-area basis                    : particle-surface estimate from Frederickson et al. (2007), Table 3
  Table 3 windrow fractions            : > 40 mm 9.8%, 20-40 mm 26.6%, 10-20 mm 26.6%, < 10 mm 37.0%

  Representative diameters             : > 40 mm 60.0 mm, 20-40 mm 30.0 mm, 10-20 mm 15.0 mm, < 10 mm 5.0 mm
  Equivalent particle diameter         : d32 = 8.01 mm from 1/d32 = sum(w_i / d_i)
  External area per fill volume        : 6/d32 = 749.3 m^2/m^3

                                         assumes the Table 3 mass fractions represent equal-density
                                         agglomerate classes, so the external particle area follows

  Velocity-limit interpretation        : there is no single fixed hole-velocity limit independent of
